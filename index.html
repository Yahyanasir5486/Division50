Strategic insights & recommended actions
Critical — call answer rate is near zero
Only 4 of 1,131 call attempts were answered (0.35%). Cold calling unknown numbers in KSA is near-universally rejected. WhatsApp, LinkedIn voice notes, or email warm-up before calls are table-stakes in this market.
Qualification rate is excellent — product-market fit is strong
88.2% of meeting attendees qualify. The pitch and value proposition are landing well. The entire problem is getting prospects to pick up and show up, not the product itself.
3rd-attempt follow-through is severely lacking
76% of contacts received only 1 call. Industry benchmarks require 5–8 touchpoints for B2B conversion. The drop from 2nd (217) to 3rd (15) attempts represents a massive missed opportunity.
Real Estate & Retail are clear winning verticals
Both verticals deliver 2.9–3.1% conversion rates vs. 0.8% in Hospitality. Budget should be reallocated heavily toward these two industries for maximum ROI.
40% of booked meetings are not being attended
Cancellations, no-shows, and reschedules account for 10 of 25 booked meetings. Automated 24hr + 2hr reminders via WhatsApp and calendar invites should recover at least half these drop-outs.
Targeting too many small companies
75% of the database has under 200 employees. For enterprise software like Infrability, these likely have smaller budgets. Filtering for 200+ employee companies should increase deal size significantly.
Hospitality is high volume, low yield
120 calls to Hospitality produced only 1 lead (0.8%). Either the contact list quality is low or the segment has lower pain alignment with Infrability's offering. Consider pausing.
Strong ICP signal from decision-maker roles
IT Managers, CEOs, COOs, and Finance heads account for the majority of leads. This ICP should be enforced across the entire 906-contact call list and used to build the LinkedIn targeting strategy.
